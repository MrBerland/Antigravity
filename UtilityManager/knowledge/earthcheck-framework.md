# EarthCheck Framework — Utility Manager Reference
## Kerzner International / One & Only Cape Town

## What is EarthCheck?
EarthCheck is the world's leading scientific benchmarking and certification program for sustainable travel and tourism. It is science-backed, third-party verified, and aligned with Agenda 21 principles.

**Kerzner International made EarthCheck certification MANDATORY across its entire portfolio in 2019.** One & Only Cape Town is therefore EarthCheck certified and must meet all KPI requirements annually.

Certification levels: **Benchmarked → Silver → Gold → Platinum → Master**
One & Only Reethi Rah (Maldives) achieved Platinum in 2025 — this is the benchmark ambition.

## Mandatory KPIs Tracked by EarthCheck

### 1. Energy Consumption
- **Metric:** kWh per guest night (primary) + Total kWh (absolute)
- **Activity Measure:** Guest nights (when occupancy data available)
- **Benchmark bands:** Baseline (above average) and Best Practice (exemplary)
- **Agent KPIs:**
  - Total electricity consumed (kWh/day, monthly, YoY)
  - Electricity base load (kW, 02:00–05:00 window)
  - Energy Use Intensity (kWh/m² where floor area known)

### 2. Potable Water Consumption
- **Metric:** m³ per guest night
- **Climate Zone Benchmarks (Mediterranean — Cape Town):**

  | Band | m³/guest night |
  |------|----------------|
  | Excellent | < 0.60 |
  | Satisfactory | 0.60–0.75 |
  | High | 0.75–1.10 |
  | Excessive | > 1.10 |

- **Agent KPIs:**
  - Total water consumed (kL/day, monthly, YoY)
  - Water base load (L/hr, 02:00–05:00 window) — leak detection signal
  - m³/guest night (activated when occupancy data provided)

### 3. Greenhouse Gas Emissions (GHG)
- **Metric:** kgCO₂e per guest night + total tonnes CO₂e
- **Scope 2:** Purchased electricity (0.93 kgCO₂e/kWh — Eskom NGER 2023/24)
- **Scope 1:** Direct gas combustion (1.884 kgCO₂e/m³ natural gas)
- **Carbon Tax Exposure:** ZAR 236/tonne CO₂e (2025)
- **Agent KPIs:**
  - Monthly Scope 1 + Scope 2 emissions (kgCO₂e)
  - Carbon tax cost (ZAR)
  - YoY emissions trend

### 4. Waste (Outside Augos scope — for reference)
- Waste to landfill (kg/guest night)
- Recycled/reused/composted (%)

### 5. Social & Community (Outside Augos scope — for reference)
- Local employment ratio
- Community investment

## Reporting Requirements

EarthCheck requires:
1. **Annual data submission** for certification renewal
2. **Third-party independent audit** verification
3. **Continuous improvement** — year-on-year reduction in intensity metrics

The Utility Manager agent automates the data collection and monitoring aspects,
generating monthly EarthCheck-aligned reports for the Sustainability Officer.

## Net Positive Hospitality (World Sustainable Hospitality Alliance)
Kerzner joined the WSHA on **22 April 2025**, committing to "Net Positive Hospitality":
- **Pillar 1 — Planet:** Efficient resource use, pollution minimisation, biodiversity
- **Pillar 2 — People:** Fair wages, community empowerment, cultural preservation
- **Pillar 3 — Place:** Local economy, heritage, sense of place
- **Pillar 4 — Prosperity:** Long-term economic sustainability

From a utility perspective, "Net Positive" means the property should:
- Move towards net-zero and eventually net-positive energy (generation > consumption)
- Reduce water intensity year-on-year
- Minimise carbon emissions and offset residual emissions
