Consumption Breakdown

Visual attribution of energy consumption across the full meter hierarchy. The interactive tree diagram exposes exactly where energy flows — and critically, where it doesn't, through "Not measured" gap detection.

1. Capability & Purpose

Knowing total site consumption is table stakes. The strategic question is: where is the energy going? Consumption Breakdown answers this by mapping every kilowatt-hour through the physical meter hierarchy — from the site root to individual sub-meters.

  • Interactive Hierarchy Tree: SVG-rendered tree from Root → Main Switches → Sub-meters with kWh values and percentage of parent.
  • "Not Measured" Detection: When the sum of child meters doesn't equal the parent, the gap is flagged in red as "Not measured" — revealing unmonitored loads, potential theft, or equipment gaps.
  • Stacked Bar + Donut Charts: Half-hourly or daily toggle showing consumption distribution across measurement points.
  • Consumption Log Table: Start/end meter readings with consumption per meter (15 rows at One & Only).
The Smoking Gun

The "Not measured" values at One & Only Cape Town (18.77% and 16.51%) are a smoking gun. Nearly 35% of energy consumption is passing through the main switches but isn't attributed to any sub-meter. This is either unmonitored equipment (opportunity to add metering), electrical losses (investigation needed), or potentially unauthorised consumption. Either way, it's invisible without this tree view.
