Power Factor & Demand

The most financially impactful energy report. Quantifies the exact cost of suboptimal power factor, tracks 24 months of demand peaks, and provides system status with correction recommendations — turning abstract electrical concepts into concrete capital expenditure business cases.

1. Capability & Purpose

Power factor is the ratio of useful power (kW) to total apparent power (kVA). A PF below 0.96 means the facility is drawing more current than necessary — creating heat losses in cables, transformers, and motors, and incurring utility penalties. This report quantifies the financial impact and prescribes the solution.

  • 24-Month Default Range: The longest default view in the platform — because PF degradation is a slow trend that requires long-term perspective.
  • 24-Month Hero Chart: Combo chart with bars (PF lagging/leading) + lines (Power at Peak, Peak Demand, Reactive Power).
  • Financial Loss Quantification: Actual Loss (cumulative) and Potential Loss (if PF were corrected) — the gap between them is the business case.
  • System Status Card: "Under Capacity — Requires Attention, 329.42 kVAr" — a prescription, not just a diagnosis.

2. The Four KPI Cards

PF Performance 0.96 Current average power factor — declining from 0.99 (a warning trend)

The Business Case in One Card

The system status card reads "Under Capacity — System Requires Attention — 329.42 kVAr". This single card is a business case: install 329 kVAr of capacitor banks (~R150,000 CapEx) and eliminate R31,000/year in penalties, achieving payback in under 5 years. The "Potential Loss" column even models what savings would look like if PF were corrected.
