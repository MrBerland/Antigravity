Downtime Report & Hierarchy

The most analytically powerful diagnostic tool in the manufacturing module. It forces the quantification of ambiguous stopping events into a strict 3-level categorical ontology, exposing exactly which sub-systems are destroying plant availability.

1. 3-Level Reason Hierarchy

When an unclassified delay occurs in production, simply labeling it as "Awaiting Material" or "Maintenance Issue" prevents meaningful aggregation over time. The Downtime Report mandates a normalized ontological data structure. Operators and automated PLC triggers must map stops to three explicitly constrained dimensions:

Level 1: Category (Primary Node)  // e.g., Unplanned Delay
└─ Level 2: Sub-Category (Domain)  // e.g., Logistics / Flow
└─ Level 3: Specific Reason (Fault)  // e.g., Flour Bin Full
Root Cause Triage

By enforcing this structure, Augos instantly identifies whether a production line is constrained by mechanical failure (Maintenance domain), starvation/bottlenecking (Logistics domain), or procedural inefficiency (Operations domain). This immediately settles inter-departmental conflict over who owns the production loss.

2. Pareto Mechanics & Time Distribution

Crucially, Frequency does not equal Severity. The Downtime Report automatically analyzes the hierarchical data using a Pareto sorting algorithm based on cumulative duration, not absolute count. For example, a wrapper jaw jamming 19 times for 45 seconds is frustrating, but a scheduled CIP washout that runs 4 hours over budget destroys capacity.

Category vs Factor Count Duration Pareto Impact
Pneumatic Airlock Reset 19 (High Freq) 01h 12m Minor Impact (2%)
Blockage: Transfer Value D3 01 (Low Freq) 14h 45m Severe Impact (27%)
