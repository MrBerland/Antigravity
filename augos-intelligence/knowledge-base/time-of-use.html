Time of Use

Peak/Standard/Off-peak analysis revealing when energy is used and what it costs by TOU period. The 24×7 colour-coded heatmap and 7-line daily distribution chart transform time-based tariff complexity into instant visual intelligence for load-shifting decisions.

1. Capability & Purpose

South African tariffs charge different rates depending on when energy is consumed. Time of Use deconstructs consumption and cost by TOU period — revealing hidden patterns that drive load-shifting opportunities worth tens of thousands of Rands per month.

  • Consumption vs Cost Donuts: Standard 40%, Peak 15%, Off-peak 45% — but in cost terms, Peak jumps to 23% despite being the smallest volume.
  • 24×7 Heatmap: 24 rows × 7 columns (hours × days), colour-coded green→red. Four tab views: Consumption Heatmap, Energy Cost Heatmap, TOU Consumption, TOU Cost.
  • Daily Distribution Trend: 7-line chart (one per day of week, 00:00–24:00) — Sunday clearly elevated above all others at One & Only.
  • TOU Breakdowns Table: 12-month history with Season (High/Low) column and average cost per kWh tracking seasonal impact.
The Peak Premium Exposed

Peak is 15% of consumption but 23% of cost. The rate differential (R3.34 vs R1.76 = 90% premium) makes load-shifting enormously valuable. Sunday is the highest consumption day (hotel leisure peak) but the cheapest per kWh (R1.79). "Why not schedule heavy laundry loads for Sunday when rates are lowest?" — that single scheduling change could shift thousands of kWh from Peak to Off-peak.
