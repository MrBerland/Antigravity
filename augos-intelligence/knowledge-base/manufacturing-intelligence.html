Manufacturing Intelligence Hub

Complete documentation for Augos Manufacturing Intelligence. This module replaces paper logbooks, standardizes data architecture, and deploys 9 distinct reports explicitly designed to expose factory bottlenecks using the Overall Equipment Effectiveness (OEE) standard.

1. The Manufacturing Data Problem

Modern production facilities run millions of dollars of hardware, yet their intelligence collection is fundamentally archaic. The data is either highly accurate but totally unanalyzable (paper logbooks or whiteboards) or strictly quantitative but devoid of human context (PLC and SCADA tags).

The Augos Solution

Augos acts as the unified ingestion point. It hooks directly into the machine's heartbeat (voltage, relays, photocells) to establish irrefutable truth about line status and cycle time. It then marries this with the ERP schedule and forces operators to rapidly categorize stoppages using a bounded semantic tree.

2. The Core Framework (OEE)

The entire module pivots on Overall Equipment Effectiveness (OEE), which decomposes macro capacity losses into three strictly defined pillars. By evaluating these pillars independently, management can apply the correct therapeutic intervention.

OEE % Range Classification Operational Implication
≥ 85% World Class Top-decile performance. Process is highly reliable and highly capable. Focus shifts to minor continuous improvement and energy optimization.
60–84% Typical Room for significant margin improvement. Machine likely suffers from poor layout routing, uncertified operator variance, or sporadic failure.
≤ 59% Unacceptable High-risk volatility. Process suffers from starvation, major catastrophic mechanical breakdowns, or severe downstream bottlenecking.

3. Six Dimensions of Visibility

To fully optimize a factory line, data must be interrogated through six distinct vectors. A 65% OEE is meaningless without dimensional correlation.

  • Time (Chronology): Does OEE degrade on Sundays? Do we consistently lose capacity between 03:00 and 04:00 AM?
  • Equipment (Topology): Is the constraint at the filler, the capper, or the palletizer?
  • People (Operators): Does Operator A run the line 15% faster than Operator B without increasing waste? Let's codify Operator A's method.
  • Product (SKU): Does changing over to a 2L bottle inherently destabilize the pneumatic lifter compared to the 1L bottle?
  • Causation (Reason): Why exactly did the pneumatic lifter fail? (Requires strict hierarchy).
  • Severity (Impact): Which failure mode stole 40 hours of production time this month, regardless of whether it hit 1 time or 100 times?

5. High-Impact ROI Audit

During the Pioneer Foods B-Mill audit over a 54-day observation window, the module ingested telemetry for 158 stops impacting the main feed line.

The B-Mill Findings

Standard operator feedback suggested the milling equipment was mechanically inadequate. However, the Downtime Pareto explicitly grouped 45.8% of all downtime to a single Level-3 fault: "Flour Bin Full."

The intelligence proved that the mechanical mill was highly capable, but was starved by downstream packaging bottlenecks. The resulting decision prevented a multi-million Rand mechanical expansion project in favor of optimizing downstream clearance rates.
