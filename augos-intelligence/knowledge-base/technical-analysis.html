Technical Analysis

Deep technical inspection of all measurement points with granular statistics, per-phase demand breakdown (A/B/C), reactive power (kVAr), and demand profiling. The electrical engineer's diagnostic console.

1. Capability & Purpose

Technical Analysis goes deeper than the Dashboard into the electrical characteristics of the supply. While the Dashboard confirms "is everything OK?", Technical Analysis answers "what specifically is happening at each measurement point?"

  • Per-Phase Demand Breakdown: View Phase A (Red), Phase B (White), and Phase C (Blue) demand individually — the only report that exposes phase imbalance.
  • Reactive Power Visibility: kVAr measurements quantify power that oscillates without doing useful work — a direct contributor to utility penalties.
  • Statistics Toggle: An expandable statistics panel reveals Consumption Total, Demand Maximum (with timestamp), Power at Peak, Reactive Power at Peak, and Reactive Energy Total.
Phase Imbalance Detection

An electrical engineer suspects phase imbalance is causing transformer overheating. They open Technical Analysis, expand the Statistics table, and see Phase A at 295.9 kVA vs Phase B and C at lower values. Confirmed — the building's single-phase loads are concentrated on Phase A. They redistribute circuits to balance the load, reducing cable losses and extending transformer life.
