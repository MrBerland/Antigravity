Cost Breakdown

Financial dissection of the electricity bill by tariff charge type. Seven distinct cost components are decomposed with 12-month sparklines, sub-measurement cost attribution, and four trend charts — transforming a single invoice number into an actionable financial strategy.

1. Capability & Purpose

A monthly electricity bill arrives as a single number. Cost Breakdown deconstructs that number into its seven constituent charge types, revealing which components are growing, which are controllable, and where the highest-leverage savings opportunities exist.

  • Cost Distribution Bar: Horizontal stacked bar showing % split by charge type (Standard 36.3%, Off-peak 34.3%, Peak 20.9%, Demand 6.8%, etc.)
  • Cost Breakdown Table: Per-charge line items with 12-month sparklines showing trend direction. Toggle: Fixed Charges Included/Excluded.
  • Sub Measurement Cost Breakdown: Per-sub-meter proportional cost allocation.
  • 4 Trend Charts: Cost (ZAR), Aggregate kWh Cost, Consumption (kWh), Demand (kVA) — each showing 12-month history.

2. Tariff Structure Exposed

The following table was captured live from One & Only Cape Town (January 2026), on the City of Cape Town Large Power User (Medium Voltage) TOU tariff:

Charge Units Rate (ZAR) Total (ZAR) %
Consumption — Standard 231,072 kWh 2.17/kWh 501,610 36.3%
Consumption — Off-peak 269,877 kWh 1.76/kWh 473,986 34.3%
Consumption — Peak 86,392 kWh 3.34/kWh 288,516 20.9%
Demand charge 1,234 kVA 75.89/kVA 93,619 6.8%
Network access 1,234 kVA 17.47/kVA 21,550 1.6%
Service charge 1 month 2,405/m 2,405 0.2%
Feed-in credit −0.01 kWh 1.15/kWh −0.02 0.0%
TOTAL R1,381,685
The Peak Premium

Peak consumption is only 15% of volume but 21% of cost. The rate differential (R3.34 Peak vs R1.76 Off-peak = 90% premium) makes load-shifting enormously valuable. Moving just 10% of peak usage to off-peak saves R2,295/month. The demand charge of R93,619 was set by a single 30-minute peak of 1,234 kVA — demand management could reduce this by 10–15%.
